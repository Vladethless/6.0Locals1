Popcorn walls or stucco gained popularity in the last century thanks to their low cost, how easily available it was, and how adaptable it was to all kinds of environments. Being available in various styles, it is not surprising that soon it became pretty common to see these stucco walls all around.

They began to be referred to as popcorn walls because of the texture that the plaster gives the wall it is applied to; it is pretty popcorn-like.

However, it was discovered later on that there might be more harmful to using these walls than good and that they are prone to causing several problems in your home later on.

This was when many companies started offering stucco removal services to get rid of these walls and upgrade to other options.

Why Is Stucco Bad For Your Home?

Asbestos

By far, the most severe problem with stucco is that it poses a health risk due to some obsolete models of it containing asbestos. If you have this problem, you must immediately take steps to apply for stucco removal without any second thoughts.

Cracks In Paint And Foundation

Stucco can be especially detrimental because of how easily it cracks. This is because it tends to trap moisture. It is a big no-no in soil areas high in clay and can move foundations, but even in areas with relatively firm foundations, you will notice that eventually, cracks appear.

This is even more annoying when you combine it with the fact that it is hard to paint over stucco in the first place because of its rocky, uneven surface.

Increased Risk Of Mold And Rotting

Because stucco is so good at absorbing and trapping moisture, it can often lead to molding developing in the area and starting to rot. This means it requires regular upkeep and maintenance to ensure it is not accumulating any mold. Our team of experts provides stable maintenance solutions.

Harder To Clean

This is a more common day-to-day inconvenience you will notice when working with stucco. It is much more challenging to clean than a smooth wall because you have to try to get into all the nooks and crannies to clean the textured wall. But, don’t worry. Potentially Decreased Resale Value

Stucco might look unappealing to many eyes now. This might not seem like a substantial problem upfront, but when you plan to sell or rent the house, its resale value is decreased because stucco can be considered out of style and because of the other health issues posed.
